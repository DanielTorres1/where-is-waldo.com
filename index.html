History & Publications

Where's Waldo? (originally published as Where's Wally? in the United Kingdom) is a world-famous series of children's puzzle books created by English illustrator Martin Handford in 1987. The franchise is known for its intricate double-page spread illustrations depicting massive crowd scenes. Readers are challenged to find Waldo, dressed in his signature red-and-white-striped shirt, bobble hat, and round glasses, hidden among the chaotic surrounding characters.

First released on June 25, 1987 by Walker Books in London, the books became a global phenomenon, selling over 73 million copies worldwide in 26 languages and 50 countries. Over the years, the illustrations became progressively more challenging as Handford decreased Waldo's size on the page and expanded the complexity of the crowd scenes from 225 characters in early drawings to over 850 in later titles.

The Book Collections

The franchise comprises seven primary puzzle books that form the core collection, alongside numerous activity books, poster books, and sticker sheets:

  • Where's Wally? / Where's Waldo? (1987) - The original book that introduced the world to Waldo.
  • Where's Wally Now? (1988) - Waldo travels through different historical eras.
  • Where's Wally? The Fantastic Journey (1989) - A quest across magical and fantasy lands.
  • Where's Wally in Hollywood? (1993) - Seek-and-find adventures set in different film genres.
  • Where's Wally? The Wonder Book (1997) - A meta-exploration of books, illustrations, and art.
  • Where's Wally? The Great Picture Hunt! (2006) - A combination of new scenes and classic puzzle posters.
  • Where's Wally? The Incredible Paper Chase (2009) - The seventh primary book, featuring intricate paper-chase settings.

Tips for Finding Characters

  • Waldo: Look for red and white horizontal stripes with a red and white bobble hat
  • Woof: Only his tail is visible - look for a small red and white striped tail
  • Wenda: Similar to Waldo but with glasses and different clothing
  • Whitebeard: Long white beard and wizard's robe
  • Odlaw: Yellow and black stripes - the opposite of Waldo
